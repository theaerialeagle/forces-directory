The weak force, or weak interactions, are caused by W+, W-, and Z0 bosons. Bosons are exchange particles that interact with all elementary particles in the Standard Model, and are of two types. Those types are charge-changing (CC) and neutral.

The charge-changing interactions involve W+ and W- bosons, while the neutral weak force involves Z0 bosons. CC weak interactions only occur between left particles or right antiparticles, while neutral interactions happen between both left and right particles.
